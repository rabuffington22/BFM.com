What We Treat

Types of Anxiety Disorders

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Persistent, excessive worry about everyday things -- work, health, family, finances -- that feels difficult to control and interferes with daily life. Physical symptoms like muscle tension, restlessness, and difficulty sleeping are common.

Social Anxiety

Intense fear of social situations where you might be judged or embarrassed. It can make work presentations, casual conversations, and everyday interactions feel overwhelming.

Panic Disorder

Recurrent, unexpected panic attacks -- sudden surges of intense fear with physical symptoms like racing heart, chest tightness, shortness of breath, and dizziness. The fear of the next attack can become its own source of anxiety.

Health Anxiety

Excessive worry about having or developing a serious illness. Normal body sensations get misinterpreted as signs of something dangerous, leading to constant checking and reassurance-seeking.

Our Approach

How We Help

We take an integrated approach to anxiety treatment, combining medication management with lifestyle strategies and therapy coordination:

  • Thorough evaluation -- We assess your symptoms, rule out medical causes (thyroid, cardiac, medication side effects), and understand your specific anxiety pattern
  • Medication management -- When appropriate, we prescribe SSRIs, SNRIs, buspirone, or other evidence-based medications and monitor closely for effectiveness and side effects
  • Therapy coordination -- We maintain relationships with local therapists specializing in CBT and other anxiety-focused treatments and can facilitate referrals
  • Lifestyle guidance -- Practical strategies for exercise, sleep, caffeine reduction, breathing techniques, and stress management
  • Ongoing support -- Regular follow-up visits to track progress, adjust treatment, and provide continuity of care

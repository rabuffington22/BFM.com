You Deserve to Feel Better

Depression goes far beyond feeling sad. It is a complex medical condition that affects how you think, feel, and function. It can steal your energy, your motivation, your ability to enjoy things you once loved, and make even simple daily tasks feel overwhelming.

The good news is that depression is highly treatable. At Buffington Family Medicine, we provide compassionate, judgment-free care that meets you where you are. Whether this is your first time seeking help or you have been managing depression for years, we are here to support you.

Recognizing Depression

Signs That It Might Be More Than a Bad Day

Persistent Sadness

A low mood that lasts most of the day, nearly every day, for two weeks or more. It may feel like a heavy weight you cannot shake.

Loss of Interest

Activities you once enjoyed -- hobbies, socializing, even food -- no longer bring pleasure or seem worth the effort.

Fatigue & Low Energy

Feeling exhausted despite adequate sleep. Even small tasks like showering or making a meal can feel like climbing a mountain.

Sleep Changes

Sleeping too much or too little. Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or waking up much earlier than normal.

Difficulty Concentrating

Brain fog, trouble making decisions, forgetfulness, and difficulty focusing at work or home.

Physical Symptoms

Unexplained headaches, body aches, digestive problems, or appetite changes that do not respond to typical treatment.

Treatment Options

How We Help

We take an individualized approach to depression treatment, recognizing that what works for one person may not work for another:

  • Medication management -- SSRIs (Zoloft, Lexapro, Prozac), SNRIs (Effexor, Cymbalta), and other antidepressants, carefully selected based on your symptoms and health profile
  • Therapy coordination -- We work with local therapists specializing in CBT, interpersonal therapy, and other evidence-based approaches
  • Treatment-resistant options -- For depression that has not responded to first-line treatments, we explore alternative medications and combination strategies
  • Lifestyle interventions -- Exercise prescriptions, sleep optimization, nutrition guidance, and stress reduction techniques that support recovery
  • Regular monitoring -- Frequent check-ins during the early treatment phase, with ongoing support as you stabilize

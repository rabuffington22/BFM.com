Recognizing ADHD

Common Signs & Symptoms

Difficulty Sustaining Attention

Trouble staying focused on tasks, easily distracted, frequently losing track of conversations or assignments.

Disorganization

Chronic difficulty with time management, missed deadlines, cluttered spaces, and trouble keeping track of responsibilities.

Impulsivity

Acting without thinking, interrupting others, making quick decisions without considering consequences, difficulty waiting.

Restlessness

Feeling internally driven or "on edge." In adults, this often shows up as mental restlessness rather than physical hyperactivity.

Emotional Dysregulation

Quick temper, frustration sensitivity, mood swings, and difficulty managing emotional responses proportionally.

Inconsistent Performance

Brilliant in some areas, struggling in others. Able to hyperfocus on interesting tasks but unable to start boring ones.

Our Approach

Evaluation & Treatment

We take ADHD seriously and follow a thorough evaluation process before arriving at a diagnosis or starting treatment:

  • Comprehensive evaluation -- Detailed symptom history, validated screening tools, assessment of how symptoms affect daily functioning, and ruling out other conditions
  • Medication management -- When appropriate, we prescribe stimulant and non-stimulant medications, starting low and adjusting carefully based on response
  • Behavioral strategies -- Practical tools for organization, time management, and habit-building that complement medication
  • Academic/workplace support -- Guidance on accommodations and documentation for school or work when needed
  • Regular follow-up -- Ongoing monitoring to optimize medication, address side effects, and adapt strategies as your life changes

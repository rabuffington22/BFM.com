Common Questions

Testosterone FAQs

What is Low T replacement therapy?

Testosterone Replacement Therapy (TRT) is a medically supervised treatment aimed at increasing low testosterone levels in individuals experiencing symptoms of testosterone deficiency. These symptoms can include fatigue, muscle weakness, decreased libido, and mood changes.

How do I know if I need TRT?

Simple blood tests can tell us if your testosterone level is low enough that it may be the cause of your symptoms.

What are my treatment options for TRT?

While injections are usually the most effective option for most men, we also offer prescriptions for patches or gels. Some clinics may offer pellets, but we have found that it is very difficult to get similar results to the other methods.

How much will this cost?

Because fatigue has such a dramatic effect on your overall health, insurance will usually cover these visits like a regular office visit.

How often do I have to come in?

For patients who choose to do self-injections at home, we check blood work every three months and follow up with an office visit to discuss your progress. For patients who choose to come in for injections, we follow the same schedule and they stop in weekly for injections.

Types of Insomnia

Understanding Your Sleep Issues

Difficulty Falling Asleep

Lying in bed for 30 minutes or more unable to fall asleep. Racing thoughts, physical tension, or an overactive mind keeping you from drifting off.

Difficulty Staying Asleep

Waking up multiple times during the night and struggling to fall back asleep. This fragmented sleep leaves you exhausted even after eight hours in bed.

Early Morning Waking

Waking up hours before your alarm with no ability to fall back asleep. Often associated with depression, anxiety, or stress.

Non-Restorative Sleep

Getting enough hours of sleep but still waking up unrefreshed. Your sleep quality is poor even when the quantity seems adequate.

Our Approach

How We Help You Sleep

We take a systematic approach to insomnia, starting with the least invasive interventions and building from there:

  • Sleep assessment -- Detailed evaluation of your sleep patterns, habits, medical history, and lifestyle factors that may be contributing
  • Root cause identification -- Testing for thyroid disorders, sleep apnea, hormonal imbalances, medication interactions, and mental health conditions
  • Sleep hygiene education -- Practical, evidence-based changes to your environment, habits, and routines that can dramatically improve sleep quality
  • CBT-I referrals -- Cognitive behavioral therapy for insomnia is the gold-standard first-line treatment, and we connect you with trained therapists
  • Medication management -- When appropriate, we prescribe sleep medications judiciously, with a focus on short-term use while addressing underlying causes

Sleep Hygiene Basics

Consistent schedule -- Same bedtime and wake time daily

Cool, dark room -- 65-68°F is optimal

Screen curfew -- No devices 1 hour before bed

Limit stimulants -- No caffeine after 2 PM

Common Questions

Insomnia FAQs

What is insomnia?

Insomnia is a sleep disorder where you have trouble falling asleep, staying asleep, or getting restful sleep. To qualify as insomnia, it must also be affecting your life in a negative way.

Sometimes I can't sleep. Is this insomnia?

This difficulty sleeping has to happen regularly to be considered insomnia. Generally speaking, it should have been going on for at least a month and happen at least 3 times per week.

What is the best way to treat insomnia?

Treatment can range from things as simple as following a nighttime routine to medication. We usually try to start with the simplest and safest treatment that we think will work in your particular case. This can be a very complex decision and it's something that we help people with every day. We would love to be able to help you too.

Does sleep medication work right away?

There are different types of sleep medication. Most work quickly, as in the first night you try them.

Would I have to take sleep medication every night?

No. Sleep medication is usually only taken as needed.

If I start sleep medication, do I have to take it forever?

No, you don't. You will only take the medication as long as you feel like you need it.
